About

What is Mecasimetra?

Mecasimetra is an engineering systems entity specializing in continuity-first control software built on the Kappology framework. It is both a set of principles and a practical engineering practice — where every system is measured, governed, and audited through κ, λ, and β_c.

κ
Continuity-First Design

Every system component is designed with κ (kappa) as the primary health metric. If you cannot measure continuity, you cannot govern it.

λ
Hazard Rate Instrumentation

Real-time λ (lambda) pipelines expose the instantaneous hazard rate so operators and automated systems can act before accumulation crosses β_c.

β_c
Hard Governance Thresholds

β_c = 2.0 is a non-negotiable boundary. When λ exceeds it, the system automatically pauses, sheds load, or triggers a retrain cycle — no human intervention required.

⌬
CodexRoxas — Community AI Agent

CodexRoxas is a community fork of OpenAI's Codex — a lightweight, open-source AI coding agent that runs locally, combining ChatGPT-level reasoning with sandboxed code execution and file manipulation. Apache 2.0 licensed. Not affiliated with OpenAI.

🔍
Open, Auditable, Reproducible

All Kappology-governed systems log their κ state, expose λ pipelines, and produce reproducible recovery traces. Every retrain cycle is auditable and attributable.

⚡
Minimal Dependencies

Mecasimetra systems are built with minimal external dependencies — reducing attack surface, improving portability, and keeping hazard accumulation from third-party code predictable.

Founder

Engineer & Architect

Kappology was created by a single engineer's drive to formalize what reliability engineering had long described informally — continuity as a measurable, governable scalar.

ZS

Zechariah Slaughter

Engineer & Founder Kappology Mecasimetra Systems

Zechariah Slaughter is the engineer and founder of Kappology — a continuity-first mathematical framework for governing control systems through hazard-rate dynamics. His work formalizes the relationship between system health (κ), risk accumulation (λ), and the critical bifurcation threshold (β_c = 2.0) that separates stable operation from mandatory recovery.

"Continuity is not a property you observe after the fact — it is a scalar you govern in real time. Kappology gives you the formalism to do that."
— Zechariah Slaughter

In the tradition of Newton's application of calculus to physical mechanics — bringing rigorous formalism to previously intuitive phenomena — and Edison's systematic, iterative approach to applied engineering, Slaughter's contribution is the construction of a formal calculus of continuity: a framework where system health is not left to empirical observation but is derived, predicted, and governed by mathematical law.

Where Newton gave engineers the differential equations to model motion, and Edison demonstrated that invention is methodical iteration under constraint, Kappology provides the continuity-hazard calculus for the design of systems that must not fail silently. It is an engineering framework in that lineage: rigorous, applied, and built for real-world governance.

  • Edison
